# The Red Queens Race
> A slow sort of country! 
> ~ the red queen


I recently took the time to reread Lewis Carroll's *Alice in Wonderland* and *Through the Looking Glass*. Nearly a decade later, it's become a sort of Proustian ritual for me to reread them every now and again. Although I'm generally not a fan of fanciful names and abstractions, I'll indulge myself just a tad! One of my favorite exchanges is the ***Red Queen's Race***. It goes something like this:

> **Alice** : We’ve been sprinting for ages, slow down pls!
> **Red Queen** : Of course not! This is a fast country, child.
> ***<The Queen adjusts her crown, entirely unbothered>***
> **Alice** : Back home, running usually gets you somewhere else.
> **Red Queen** : Well here, it takes all the running you can do just to stay in the same place.
> **Alice** : ... I think I’ll just sit then.

In a book where some passages require *several* rereads, it's certainly one of the more straightforward conversations to understand. It's such a charming metaphor too! Strange, huh? People generally fall into two camps:
1. Alice needs to get with the program. Run run run!!! 
2. Alice is right. Let's just *chillllll*

But from what I've tasted of fire and desire, I hold with those who favor neither. You know, there will always a next project, a next hurdle, blah, bleh, bluh, bluh, bluh...

Why not choose to be still? :)
